# SNS Operations Guideline

Sample policy document for a fictional organization. This file contains no real company data.

## Purpose

This guideline describes sample rules for operating official social channels in a media company setting.

## Standard Posting Rules

Routine social posts should use approved public information, neutral language, and clear attribution for any referenced material. Operators should avoid speculation, implied promises, personal opinions on behalf of the organization, and ambiguous claims about rights or availability.

Before publishing, the operator should verify that the post matches the intended campaign brief, does not reveal private information, and does not reuse fan or third-party material without the required permission record.

## Sensitive Content Review

Sensitive topics include personal information, public incidents, health or safety references, minors, talent privacy, rights disputes, and rapidly changing online criticism. Sensitive posts should not be published from a single-person judgment path in this demo workflow.

The recommended demo flow is: pause publishing, classify the topic, collect source context, draft a neutral holding message if needed, and cite the relevant policy sections before finalizing any public response.

## Replies and Direct Messages

Official accounts should not confirm private facts in replies or direct messages. When a user asks for information that is not already public, the operator should use a neutral response and avoid adding details.

## Deletion and Correction

If an incorrect or risky post is published, the operator should capture the post URL, timestamp, draft text, and reason for concern before editing or deleting. Public correction language should be concise and should not introduce new unsupported facts.
