# Talent Privacy Protection Policy

Sample policy document for a fictional organization. This file contains no real company data.

## Purpose

This policy explains how staff should avoid exposing private information about performers, creators, guests, or other public-facing individuals.

## Private Information

Private information includes personal contact details, private schedules, travel details, health information, family information, home locations, identification records, and any non-public personal circumstance. Staff must not confirm, deny, quote, or hint at these details in public channels.

## Public Content Review

Before publishing content that references a talent or guest, staff should check whether the information is already public, whether the context is appropriate, and whether the content could reveal private routines or locations. Even positive content may be risky if it exposes timing or location patterns.

## Responding to Rumors

When online rumors involve private information, official accounts should not amplify the claim by repeating details. The recommended demo response is to avoid confirmation, use neutral language, and escalate to the incident response workflow if the rumor is spreading rapidly.

## Fan Interaction

Fan messages, gifts, or submissions may not be used to infer or disclose private information. Staff should separate appreciation for fan engagement from any claim about private relationships, private contact, or personal access.
