# AI Tool Usage Policy

Sample policy document for a fictional organization. This file contains no real company data.

## Purpose

This policy explains how staff in the fictional demo organization may use AI tools for internal drafting, summarization, and idea exploration. AI tools may support work, but they must not replace human review, policy judgment, or source verification.

## Approved Demo Use Cases

Staff may use AI tools to draft internal outlines, rewrite non-confidential text, summarize approved sample materials, and generate brainstorming options for campaign planning. AI output must be reviewed by a human before it is used in any external-facing communication.

For external social posts, press-style copy, talent-facing drafts, or partner-facing material, AI output is treated as a draft only. The final text must be checked against the relevant SNS, copyright, privacy, and incident response policies.

## Prohibited Inputs

Staff must not enter confidential business plans, private personal information, unpublished talent details, unreleased campaign materials, access credentials, security events, or non-public partner information into third-party AI tools.

If a prompt would require sensitive material, staff should rewrite the request using neutral placeholders or use an approved private workflow.

## Human Review Requirement

AI-generated output must be checked for factual accuracy, policy alignment, tone, and source support. The reviewer should confirm that the text does not imply endorsements, promises, rights ownership, or incident positions that have not been approved in the fictional policy set.

## Citation and Traceability

When AI output is used to answer a policy question, the answer should cite the source policy sections that support the response. If the knowledge base does not contain enough evidence, the assistant must refuse rather than invent a policy.
