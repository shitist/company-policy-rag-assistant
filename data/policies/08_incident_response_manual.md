# Public Incident Response Manual

Sample policy document for a fictional organization. This file contains no real company data.

## Purpose

This manual describes a fictional first-response workflow for public incidents, online criticism, misinformation, or fast-moving reputational risk in a demo media organization.

## First Response Principles

When an incident appears, staff should pause non-essential posting, preserve context, avoid speculation, and separate confirmed facts from assumptions. The first objective is to prevent additional harm while the situation is assessed.

## Initial Triage

The triage flow has four steps: identify the source, classify the risk, collect relevant public context, and decide whether a holding message is needed. Staff should not confirm private information, security details, or rights positions unless supported by the policy set.

## Holding Message

A holding message should be short, neutral, and limited to the fact that the issue is being reviewed. It should not assign blame, promise outcomes, disclose private details, or repeat unverified claims.

## Escalation Triggers

Escalation is recommended when the issue involves privacy, safety, account compromise, legal rights, minors, targeted harassment, rapidly growing criticism, or conflicting public narratives. In those cases, SNS operators should pause replies and coordinate through the fictional risk response path.

## Post-incident Review

After the incident is stable, staff should record the timeline, decisions, public messages, source materials, and lessons learned. The review should improve future playbooks without exposing internal information.
